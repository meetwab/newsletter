# America has only one real city  
# 美国只有一个真正的城市  

[View this post on the web](https://www.noahpinion.blog/p/america-has-only-one-real-city)  
[在网页上查看此帖子](https://www.noahpinion.blog/p/america-has-only-one-real-city)  

Americans who go to Tokyo or Paris or Seoul or London are often wowed by the efficient train systems, dense housing, and walkable city streets lined with shops and restaurants.  
去东京、巴黎、首尔或伦敦的美国人常常被高效的火车系统、密集的住房和两旁布满商店和餐馆的步行街所惊艳。  

And yet in these countries, many secondary cities also have these attractive features.  
然而在这些国家，许多次要城市也具备这些吸引人的特点。  

Go to Nagoya or Fukuoka, and the trains will be almost as convenient, the houses almost as dense, and the streets almost as attractive as in Tokyo.  
去名古屋或福冈，火车几乎同样方便，房屋几乎同样密集，街道几乎同样迷人，和东京不相上下。  

The U.S. is very different.  
美国则大相径庭。  

We have New York City, and that’s about it.  
我们只有纽约市，其他的就不算了。  

People from Chicago or Boston may protest that their own cities are also walkable, but transit use statistics show just how big the gap is between NYC and everybody else:  
来自芝加哥或波士顿的人可能会抗议他们自己的城市也很适合步行，但交通使用统计数据却显示出纽约市与其他城市之间的巨大差距：  

Chicago, Boston, and the rest have their old urban cores with a few train lines and some shopping streets.  
芝加哥、波士顿和其他城市都有他们的老城区，配有几条火车线路和一些购物街。  

But for the most part, even these cities are car-centric sprawl.  
但在大多数情况下，即便是这些城市也是以汽车为中心的城市扩张。  

You can also see this in the population density numbers; New York simply towers over all the rest:  
在人口密度数据中也可以看到这一点；纽约的密度远远超过其他城市：  

There’s simply no other town in America that looks and feels like NYC.  
美国根本没有其他城市看起来和感觉像纽约市。  

Some of the reasons for this are historical.  
造成这种情况的部分原因是历史因素。  

NYC became a big city before the rise of the mass-market passenger car, so it had to use transit to move people around;  
纽约市在大众市场乘用车兴起之前就已经成为大城市，因此它不得不使用公共交通来移动人们；  

many cities, like L.A., Houston, and Phoenix, saw their growth happen later.  
许多城市，如洛杉矶、休斯顿和菲尼克斯，的增长发生得较晚。  

America’s car-friendly policies, abundant land, and desire for suburban living created the car-centric development pattern that we see in many cities in the West and South today.  
美国的汽车友好政策、丰富的土地和对郊区生活的渴望，创造了我们今天在西部和南部许多城市中看到的以汽车为中心的发展模式。  

But many older cities don’t have this excuse.  
但许多老城市并没有这个借口。  

For example, take Philadelphia.  
例如，费城就是一个例子。  

In 1910, NYC was only three times bigger than [Philly](https://substack.com/redirect/18e93274-9b0f-4119-8cbc-415087b936cd?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA);  
在1910年，纽约市的规模仅是[费城](https://substack.com/redirect/18e93274-9b0f-4119-8cbc-415087b936cd?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA)的三倍；  

by 1960 it was almost four times as big, and by 2010 it was five times as big.  
到1960年，它的规模几乎是费城的四倍，到2010年则达到了五倍。  

In other words, Philadelphia had its big growth spurt earlier than NYC did, but its outcome in terms of walkability and transit is just much weaker,  
换句话说，费城的快速增长发生得比纽约市早，但在步行性和公共交通方面的结果却要弱得多，  

with fewer than 20% of Philadelphians using transit for their commute.  
使用公共交通通勤的费城居民不到20%。  

Very little of downtown Philly looks like Manhattan.  
费城市中心几乎没有地方看起来像曼哈顿。  

The reason NYC is so much bigger than every other city in America is partly mathematical — every country tends to have one city that towers over the rest in terms of total population.  
纽约市之所以比美国其他城市大得多，部分原因是数学上的 — 每个国家通常都有一个城市在人口总数上远超其他城市。  

And it’s partly economic — Ed Glaeser has a great [essay](https://substack.com/redirect/b2102691-addc-4caa-9c7e-b415189d9dd5?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA) on the industrial history of NYC.  
而部分原因是经济因素 — Ed Glaeser有一篇关于纽约市工业历史的精彩[文章](https://substack.com/redirect/b2102691-addc-4caa-9c7e-b415189d9dd5?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA)。  

But those reasons can’t explain why NYC is so much denser than other cities.  
但这些原因无法解释为什么纽约市的密度远高于其他城市。  

In fact, because NYC includes such an unusually large percent of its metropolitan area (44%, compared to less than 33% for other major cities),  
事实上，由于纽约市包含了其大都市区中异常大的一部分（44%，而其他主要城市不到33%），  

you might naively expect it to be less dense — San Francisco is just the tiny metropolitan core of the Bay Area, while NYC includes Staten Island and other outlying areas.  
你可能天真地认为它的密度会更低 — 旧金山只是湾区的小型大都市核心，而纽约市则包括斯塔滕岛和其他周边地区。  

Yet NYC is still far denser than SF or any other large American city.  
然而，纽约市的密度仍然远高于旧金山或其他任何大型美国城市。  

The reason NYC is America’s only truly dense large city is due to policy.  
纽约市之所以是美国唯一真正密集的大城市，原因在于政策。  

Other cities have restrictive zoning codes that limit floor-area [ratios](https://substack.com/redirect/bfd28169-2af1-4675-9f47-611b59f89c46?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA), impose citywide height limits, impose parking minimums, and restrict certain areas to single-family homes.  
其他城市有限制性的分区法规，限制建筑面积[比率](https://substack.com/redirect/bfd28169-2af1-4675-9f47-611b59f89c46?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA)，施加全市高度限制，设定停车最少标准，并将某些区域限制为单户住宅。  

For example, here’s a map showing just how much of San Francisco’s land (in pink) is zoned to allow only single-family homes:  
例如，这里有一张地图显示了旧金山有多少土地（粉色部分）被划为仅允许单户住宅：  

Keep in mind that this is America’s second-densest big city.  
请记住，这是美国第二密集的大城市。  

New York City really stands alone, in terms of allowing tall buildings.  
在允许高楼方面，纽约市确实独树一帜。  

New York City is also unique in having an extensive subway system.  
纽约市在拥有广泛的地铁系统方面也独具特色。  

In terms of miles of rail, NYC has more than other cities, but just as important is the shape of the network.  
在铁路里程方面，纽约市的长度超过其他城市，但同样重要的是网络的形状。  

NYC’s subway is a dense grid that covers all of Manhattan and much of Brooklyn;  
纽约市的地铁是一个密集的网格，覆盖了整个曼哈顿和大部分布鲁克林；  

other cities tend to have commuter rail systems that connect the city center directly to outlying areas but which aren’t as useful for getting around within the central city.  
其他城市往往有通勤铁路系统，直接将市中心与周边地区连接起来，但在市中心内部的交通便利性上却不如纽约市。  

For example, here are train maps for NYC, San Francisco, and Boston:  
例如，这里是纽约市、旧金山和波士顿的火车地图：  

American cities are no longer able to build subways.  
美国的城市不再能够建设地铁。  

This is partly because we’ve outlawed the cheap methods used to build them:  
这部分是因为我们已经禁止了用于廉价建造地铁的方法：  

But a lot of it is because of the same problems of low state capacity and excessive citizen [input](https://substack.com/redirect/14805d33-42a1-4fe0-a9a4-354a8b418393?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA) that block every other construction project in America.  
但很大程度上是因为低国家能力和过度的公民[参与](https://substack.com/redirect/14805d33-42a1-4fe0-a9a4-354a8b418393?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA)的问题，这些问题阻碍了美国其他所有建设项目。  

In other words, America has only one New York because no other American city wants to become like New York.  
换句话说，美国只有一个纽约，因为没有其他美国城市想要变得像纽约。  

Throughout the country, “Manhattanization” is a scary term that gets thrown at any developer who wants to increase density.  
在全国范围内，“曼哈顿化”是一个可怕的术语，任何想要增加密度的开发商都会被贴上这个标签。  

And yet the number of Americans who want to live in NYC is not small; it’s huge.  
然而，想要住在纽约市的美国人并不少；这个数字非常庞大。  

NYC 1-bedroom rents have been soaring, even as they stagnate nationwide:  
纽约市的一居室租金飙升，即使全国范围内的租金停滞不前：  

Someone wants to live in NYC, obviously.  
显然，有人想住在纽约市。  

Partly that’s because of the enormous consumption benefits for the young wealthy childless [people](https://substack.com/redirect/beda94eb-1f39-42d0-9853-58d062cfd50a?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA) who love living in cities.  
部分原因是对于那些热爱城市生活的年轻富裕无子女的[人群](https://substack.com/redirect/beda94eb-1f39-42d0-9853-58d062cfd50a?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA)来说，消费福利巨大。  

And partly that’s because dense cities allow industrial clustering [effects](https://substack.com/redirect/c9d5dae9-db24-4431-a381-0a649e08057d?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA) — everyone knows that if you want to hire good employees in banking, publishing, corporate law, and so on, it helps to be in NYC.  
部分原因是密集的城市允许工业集聚[效应](https://substack.com/redirect/c9d5dae9-db24-4431-a381-0a649e08057d?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA) — 每个人都知道，如果你想在银行、出版、公司法等领域雇佣优秀员工，身处纽约市会有很大帮助。  

Is one city enough to hold all of the Americans who want to live in big, dense cities, as well as all of the Americans who need to live there for work?  
一个城市足以容纳所有想住在大型密集城市的美国人，以及所有因工作需要住在那里的人吗？  

It is not.  
答案是否定的。  

The middle class is being pushed out of [NYC](https://substack.com/redirect/c762464f-7544-4421-b9f7-2bc6c572c900?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA) at a rapid clip.  
中产阶级正在迅速被挤出[纽约市](https://substack.com/redirect/c762464f-7544-4421-b9f7-2bc6c572c900?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA)。  

Americans are trying to pile into other cities, but NIMBYism isn’t letting those cities build many new houses to accommodate them;  
美国人试图涌入其他城市，但反对建设主义（NIMBY主义）使这些城市无法建造许多新房来容纳他们；  

as a result, rents in other cities go up faster than [wages](https://substack.com/redirect/c9aaa174-b26f-4ece-a1fe-0edbe9ab19d7?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA).  
因此，其他城市的租金上涨速度快于[工资](https://substack.com/redirect/c9aaa174-b26f-4ece-a1fe-0edbe9ab19d7?j=eyJ1IjoiMjBsbmJwIn0.KztYzEWpJOR2MnnIg5ijVYRyTJF67hinhCJnHuA6bbA)。  

America needs more than one NYC.  
美国需要的不止一个纽约市。  

It needs Chicago, Philadelphia, and other big old cities with existing walkable urban cores to step up and Manhattanize themselves,  
它需要芝加哥、费城和其他拥有现有步行城市核心的大城市，主动提升自己，进行曼哈顿化，  

so that the country won’t just have one Manhattan.  
这样国家就不会只有一个曼哈顿。  

How can this be done?  
这该如何实现呢？  

The first step is simply to adopt NYC-style big floor-area ratios, as well as all the city’s other permissive building policies.  
第一步就是简单地采用纽约市风格的大建筑面积比率，以及该市的其他宽松建筑政策。  

Allow more density, and some density will get built.  
允许更多的密度，一些密度就会被建造出来。  

The second thing these cities can do is to build more trains.  
这些城市可以做的第二件事是建设更多的火车。  

Because the “cut and cover” policies that build subways cheaply are always very unpopular,  
因为“切割和覆盖”政策以廉价建造地铁总是非常不受欢迎，  

this probably also means building elevated trains and surface rail.  
这可能也意味着建设高架火车和地面铁路。  

NIMBYism will have to be overcome, but that’s true of just about anything that anyone wants to get done.  
反对建设
